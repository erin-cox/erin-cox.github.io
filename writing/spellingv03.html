The idea is to take the phonemic British RP pronunciations from the Oxford English Dictionary and translate the IPA symbols into something more natural, ignoring things like stress and aspiration and attempting to preserve the look and feel of written English.

Spelling rules

The vowels are as follows. (The use of w as a vowel is taken from Welsh.) Some of these vowels are pronounced differently in different dialects.

Apostrophes are used to separate letters in a digraph pronounced separately:

Syllabic consonants are written with a u before the consonant:

Standard New
mansion manshun
dazzle dazul
navigable navigerbul
Lastly:
  • The word “the” is written “dher” before consonants and “dhí” before vowels, reflecting the pronunciation.
  • Existing spelling conventions such as capitalised “I”, possessive “’s”, and apostrophes in contractions such as “I’ve” and “she’d” are ignored.
  • Rules behind capitalisation and other punctuation (including hyphens) remain unchanged.

The North Wind and the Sun

The North Wind and the Sun were disputing which was the stronger, when a traveler came along wrapped in a warm cloak. They agreed that the one who first succeeded in making the traveler take his cloak off should be considered stronger than the other. Then the North Wind blew as hard as he could, but the more he blew the more closely did the traveler fold his cloak around him; and at last the North Wind gave up the attempt. Then the Sun shined out warmly, and immediately the traveler took off his cloak. And so the North Wind was obliged to confess that the Sun was the stronger of the two.

Practical considerations

No new glyphs are introduced. The accented characters required are Áá Éé Íí Óó Ẃẃ, all of which can be found on existing Welsh keyboard layouts and in many popular fonts.

Capitalisation and punctuation standards remain unchanged.

Given the prior existence of a phonemic dictionary (the OED) and the regular nature of the spelling, this proposal is highly amenable to automation, with a simple dictionary implementation able to handle all but a small number of homographs such as “lead”, “close” and “wind”.

The alphabet is simply abcdefghijlmnoprstuvwyz.
