An elegant problem from the International Mathematical Olympiad.

Question

There are 4n pebbles of weights $1,2,\ldots,4n$. Each pebble is coloured in one of $n$ colours and there are four pebbles of each colour. Show that we can arrange the pebbles into two piles so that the following two conditions are both satisfied:
  • The total weights of both piles are the same.
  • Each pile contains two pebbles of each colour.
